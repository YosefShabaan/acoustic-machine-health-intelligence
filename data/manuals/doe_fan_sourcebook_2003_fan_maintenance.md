This tracked file is a compact AMHI RAG source note derived from an official
DOE fan-system sourcebook. It is not a replacement for the full sourcebook and
must be used only as retrieval evidence for cautious inspection-oriented
communication.

## DOE-FAN-2003-SYSTEM-APPROACH

The sourcebook frames fan maintenance as a system problem, not only an isolated
component problem. Inspection should consider current operating conditions,
load duty, process needs, energy use, monitoring history, and whether the fan is
still operating near the intended duty point. AMHI may use this section to
support recommendations to compare acoustic findings with operating records and
system conditions.

## DOE-FAN-2003-BASIC-MAINTENANCE

The sourcebook describes reactive fan maintenance as common but limited. Audible
mechanical noise, poor performance, or vibration should not be treated as a
confirmed fault by AMHI; instead, they should trigger inspection planning. The
source supports checking fan cleaning needs, lubrication practices, and whether
observed noise or vibration has changed relative to normal operation.

## DOE-FAN-2003-VIBRATION-MONITORING

The sourcebook treats vibration monitoring as a practical maintenance aid for
rotating fan and motor equipment. Vibration measurements can be compared with a
baseline taken when equipment was commissioned or known to be operating normally.
AMHI may recommend comparing current acoustic evidence with available vibration
or operating-history records, but must not claim that acoustic evidence alone
confirms bearing, winding, imbalance, or drivetrain faults.

## DOE-FAN-2003-RECORDS

The sourcebook emphasizes written logs for observations, inspection results,
repairs, adjustments, and operating conditions under which a problem is noticed.
AMHI may recommend recording the event identity, acoustic observation, operating
condition, inspection findings, and follow-up action so later changes can be
trended. The recommendation remains recordkeeping and inspection support, not a
diagnosis.

## DOE-FAN-2003-COMMON-FAN-PROBLEMS

The sourcebook groups fan problems into fan/motor assembly problems and system
problems. It notes that wear can affect bearings, belt drives, blade surfaces,
and motors over time. AMHI may use this section to suggest a broad inspection of
fan/motor assembly condition and system context after abnormal acoustic noise,
while avoiding any statement that one component is confirmed to have failed.

## DOE-FAN-2003-BELT-DRIVES

The sourcebook identifies belt drives as maintenance-intensive. Belt wear,
slippage, insufficient tension, excessive tension, uneven loading, and
contaminant buildup can affect performance, noise, vibration, reliability, and
downtime. AMHI may recommend inspection of belt condition, tension, alignment,
and cleanliness when retrieved evidence supports it.

## DOE-FAN-2003-NOISE-VIBRATION-INDICATORS

The sourcebook connects inefficient or oversized fan operation with high
airborne noise, structure-borne vibration, stall-related pulsing airflow noise,
duct movement, and vibrating supports. AMHI may recommend checking whether high
noise or vibration coincides with operating point, airflow restrictions, damper
position, duct/support condition, and other system-level indicators.

## Limits For AMHI Use

- Use as approved public source evidence for fan inspection context.
- Do not use as a root-cause classifier.
- Do not convert acoustic rank scores into confidence or probability.
- Do not claim remaining life or time to failure.
- Do not claim production maintenance validation without later evaluation.
